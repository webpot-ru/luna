Standing Spanish A1 workbook rule from 2026-06-09: final buyer-facing delivery mirrors the Oxford US/UK pattern and produces separate edition workbooks, not one mixed Spanish sheet:

- Latin American Spanish workbook: primary `ES-419`, `ES` omitted from the buyer-facing main sheet and kept in `Card Metadata`, source artifacts and isolated `spanish_a1_*` tables.
- Spanish (Spain) workbook: primary `ES`, `ES-419` omitted from the buyer-facing main sheet and kept in `Card Metadata`, source artifacts and isolated `spanish_a1_*` tables.
- Each edition main sheet has 108 columns: 53 display columns, 53 example columns, primary Spanish word transcription and primary Spanish example transcription.
- Spanish A1 final edition transcription columns use course-specific broad learner IPA, generated by `scripts/spanish-a1/lib/spanish-pronunciation.mjs` from the visible word/example in the concrete edition. Spain `ES` and Latin American Spanish `ES-419` differ where the course policy differs, for example distinción `/θ/` vs seseo `/s/`. This is a Spanish A1 course override and does not change the ordinary-deck `ES`/`ES-419` native-orthography policy.
- Support-language transcription/audio remain out of scope unless a later documented contract changes that.
- A release is not final delivery-ready under this rule until both edition workbooks have local readback, native Google Sheet upload/update and Google Sheet readback.
- Final workbook and Google Sheet readbacks must resolve `Course Metadata` from the concrete edition workbook manifest or two-edition contract. They must not fall back to the Part 001 Latin American metadata source when checking Part 002 or later edition workbooks.
- `Course Metadata` must use full reader-facing edition labels, not cramped abbreviations. Required examples include `Испанский (Испания) A1`, `Испанский (Латинская Америка) A1`, `Spanish (Spain) A1` and `Latin American Spanish A1`; old shortened labels such as `Исп.`, `Лат. Ам.` and `Latin Amer.` are blocked by export/readback gates. The sheet should be styled for reading: wrapped text, wider language columns, frozen header/label column and table borders.
- Support-language display fields must never use exact dash placeholders (`-`, `–`, `—`). If a target language has no direct Spanish definite-article equivalent, use a localized grammar gloss such as "definite article, masculine singular"; Spanish A1 release/sample/expanded gates block dash placeholders in `display` / `display_translation`.

## Spanish-Specific Field Rules

Nouns:

- `article_ES` is required for Spanish nouns: usually `el` or `la`; plural-only and uncountable cases must be explicitly marked.
- `gender` is required for nouns and must be one of `masculine`, `feminine`, `common`, `invariable`, `plural_only` or `not_applicable`.
- Article/gender must be checked against Kaikki/DBnary and Spanish grammar rules.
- Stressed feminine `a` forms such as `el agua` must record feminine gender even when the article is `el`.

Verbs:

- Store the infinitive as the canonical source form unless the release row intentionally teaches a high-frequency phrase.
- Store `verb_group` as `-ar`, `-er`, `-ir`, `irregular` or `not_applicable`.
- A1 rows should prefer present-tense examples and avoid advanced tense/aspect unless the item itself requires it.

Adjectives:

- Store masculine singular as the canonical lemma when applicable.
- Note variable forms where learner-facing gender agreement matters.

Transcription:

- For Spanish A1 final edition workbooks, `ES` and `ES-419` primary word/example transcription columns use course-specific broad learner IPA (`spanish_a1_broad_learner_ipa_v1`), not orthographic copies.
- The IPA is generated from the visible learner-facing display/example in the concrete edition by `scripts/spanish-a1/lib/spanish-pronunciation.mjs`; noun word IPA includes visible `el`/`la` where present.
- Spain/Latin America pronunciation differences such as Castilian `z`/soft `c` distinción versus Latin American `seseo` are encoded when the broad learner IPA policy can represent them.
- Candidate-pool source transcription fields may store base-display IPA without learner articles; final workbook/export/readback must compute IPA from the visible workbook cells.
- This override is scoped to Spanish A1 course contracts. Ordinary decks still follow `docs/language-transcription-policy.md` default `ES`/`ES-419` native-orthography transcription unless their own contract explicitly changes it.

Examples:

- Examples are LunaCards-authored, short and concrete.
- Preferred A1 shape: one target item, one simple scene, present tense, no idioms, no abstract template sentences.
- `example_ES_419` may be identical to `example_ES` when the sentence is region-neutral; otherwise it must preserve the same scene with regional wording.
